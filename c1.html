Enhance User Experience and increase User engagement for Markid.

Client: Markid

Role: End-to-End Solo Ux/Ui Designer

Overview

Markid is a simple and enjoyable way to sell and shop for kids’ preloved items.
Markid is a PARENT marketplace where parents can connect to buy and sell kids preloved, gently used items - easily and trustfully.

Roles and responsibilities

As product designer for this project, prioritized pain points, mapped task flows,Redesign IA, designed wireframe sketches and hi fidelity mockups, prototyped and followed up with user validation.

Objective

The main objective of the redesign was to enhance the user experience and increase the user engagement. The main issue with the current app was there was no engaging factor that kept the user holding on to the app and made them come back.

Scope and constraints

Identify and evaluate the current user journey and fix it. Define project roadmap Increase user engagement Improve user experience

The users:

The main target audience were the parents with kids aged between 0-14 years. There are 2 types of users:

Buyers and Sellers

Essentially, there are 4 things that users care most about as part of their goals and motivations for using Markid: Price, Quality, Item, and Location. It is then easy to focus on these areas and ensure the app can easily answer the main question for each of them. Although parents care most about the type of item they are searching for. Like any other shopping app.

The Process:

Analyzing the existing experience Research Design Test Conclusion

Business Goal & User goals:

‘Business goals and user goals often wage a war against each other during a web design project. This actually is true for products as well. Also, this is most likely where you’re most familiar with a business goal and a user goal’

Analyzing the existing experience:

The app had no engaging factor apart from just buying and selling the items, there was no motivation for the user to come back to the platform when the task was done. The product image on the item card was not proper and the product details were not properly displayed.

Research:

I gather information about the user experience and their reviews with the help of Google play store and App store. Then I sorted the gathered information and identified the problems. It also helped me in understanding the user perspective of what exactly they wanted.

Why will people use Markid?:

To sell preloved items Users may be budget conscious and want to buy low budget items. To read about parenting blogs Get feedback for other parents on the products

User Interviews

I recruited 10 participants. Four were interviewed in-person and 4 were interviewed via video-chat. Wanting to suss out attitudes towards preloved markets, I asked about their experiences buying and selling.From my interviews, two general groups emerged:

Parents who want quality products but are on tight budgets. Circumstance-driven users, who generally tend to sell rather than buy and do so because of circumstances that cause them to downsize their belongings or otherwise get rid of certain items.

Insights and key behavior patterns mined from user interviews:

Communication was key and expected between buyers and sellers, and all participants communicated with sellers or buyers at some point during their experiences. Lack of trust in the second party when making an exchange on Markid. Frustrated by time spent coordinating a transaction. Users are budget-conscious. User where facing dicuculti in listing the items for sale There was no mechanism to save the item for later

User Personas

I was intentional about learning as much as I could about each user, beyond their habits related to secondhand goods, as these insights helped me develop a rich user persona to ground our design.

So meet Emma and Sophia

Emma was our Primary User Persona, an active buyer and seller on Markid. Sophia was our Secondary User Persona, who occasionally buys secondhand goods.

Information Architecture

Now that I had my actionable takeaways and a better understanding of my potential users, I needed to determine how information was organized on the existing app and where a new feature could be embedded smoothly into it. I created a site map using whimsical, which is used to demonstrate relationships between content within the Markid app. The site map made me realize how much information was contained within the app, and I understood how overwhelming the app could be to users.
Because I asked participants to perform a preliminary usability test during my research, I had a general idea of common user and task flows already. Using Whimsical, I created visual user and task flows from a buyer and a seller perspective.

Task Flow

For the task flows, I considered three tasks:

Make an offer Reply to buyers message Add a product for listing

Design

Low-fidelity wireframes

I add a new page as a part of user engagement in the app. Which is a personalised feed page and Watch list (tabs), now the user will be updated with the items he/she is interested in. Detail page: I restructured the detail page by filing the make an offer button and message button at the bottom of the screen so that once the user wants to buy the product they will not have to search for the CTA for the same, which was not the case in earlier design. In earlier designs users had to scroll down the screen to make an offer.

Based on the user testing results I made few changes to the wireframes and moved towards the visual design. Main focus was given to onboarding process to create personalised home page, messaging experience, selling experience and explore page.

Visual design & UX Rationale

Onboarding pages

The main intention of introducing the onboarding flow was to build a personalized home page for the user.

To begin with, the user will have to login/Sign up using their Phone number/ Amazon/google of Apple account

We take user kids' info such as the age of the kids and gender so that we can use this data to show only the items that fall under that criteria.

In the next step, we ask the user to select the category and brand they are interested in so that we avoid the dumping of product category and brand in which the user is not interested.

At the end of the process, we look for user location to show them the products available near them.

landing pages

Once onboarding the done it was time to build the user personalized home page

Here user can call only those items that he has selected in the onboarding.

We divide the home page into two sections using tab elements one My feed and the other Watch list.

My feed: Followed Categories and brands
Watch list: Liked items

Users can unfollow any category or brand by clicking on the manage button.

Explore and Detail page

Explore page was re-thought added 'items near you, handpicked, Based on your recent search' section on the explore so that users can explore the items that are out there on the platform.

Whenever a user searches for an item and the item is not available instead of showing an empty screen I decided to add a button as Follow buy clicking on it the user will be notified whenever the product is available on the platform.

Conclusion

Markid is a great platform for selling used or new items to strangers. Unlike other platforms, Markid gives a unique experience shopping in a sharing economy where trust is built due to a buyer and seller rating system and commenst for the seller. To make the buying experience smoother, I identified three major pain points that users have throughout their use of Markid as buyers. Insufficient product information visibility
Poor way finding for finding saved items
Lack of motivation for the user to stay with the app
