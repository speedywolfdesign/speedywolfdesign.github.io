Enhance User Experience and increase User engagement for Markid.

Client: Markid

Role: End-to-End Solo Ux/Ui Designer

Overview

Markid is a simple and enjoyable way to sell and shop for kids’ preloved items.
Markid is a PARENT marketplace where parents can connect to buy and sell kids preloved, gently used items - easily and trustfully.

Objective

The client wanted to revamp their existing website with a brand new variant which can meet their business goals better. The new website not only acts as a marketing tool but also has an in-built dashboard to aid students with their learning requirements

The Challenge

Designing a platform with users across different age groups and users with different use cases was an obstacle to overcome. The landing page has to evoke a sense ofplayfulness because it’s a coding for kids business. Simultaneously, the initial visitors are primarily parents and grandparents who interact with the landing page. Keeping these data points in mind to design a website which achieves both these goals was key

The Challenge

Keeping the provided personas & objectives in mind, we designed an experience that’s fun, slightly-techy and minimal from a visual perspective and a minimal & intuitive design for users to achieve their goals.

Information Architecture

Understanding the business objectives and user personas, we devised the Information Architecture best suited for each page on the site.

User Flows

Since the platform has an onboarding mechanism to convert visitors intoregistered users, achieving this result through the simplest process was key. Upon several iterations and discussions with the Codinu team, we decided on flows that are optimal.

Wireframes

After defining the Information Architecture and User Flows, we designed wireframes to showcase navigation and content across the site.

Visual Design

We created a colour palette from Codinu’s existing brand colours. We went with a fun, playful and slightly techy UI to indicate that this is a coding for kids’ business. With respect to users such as parents and grandparents, we ensured high contrast was used through the site to adhere to accessibility standards. To ensure a fun and creative feel along with good readability, we coupled Poppins along with Lato as our typefaces of choice.

Conclusion

Markid is a great platform for selling used or new items to strangers. Unlike other platforms, Markid gives a unique experience shopping in a sharing economy where trust is built due to a buyer and seller rating system and commenst for the seller. To make the buying experience smoother, I identified three major pain points that users have throughout their use of Markid as buyers. Insufficient product information visibility
Poor way finding for finding saved items
Lack of motivation for the user to stay with the app
